Drift and Diffusion

Drift

  • Charge carriers (electrons or holes) in a doped semiconductor begin to move in the direction of the electric field when it is applied across it.
  • As they are moving, they undergo collisions with vibrating Si atoms. This makes their movement unpredictable, haphazard (also known as Brownian motion).
  • However, the net velocity of each charge carrier is in the direction of the electric field.
  • Therefore, the velocity can be modeled as:
    vd = -чn * E   for electrons
    vd = чp * E   for holes
  • ч is the mobility of the electron defined by the ratio of vd and E. Mobility can be thought of as the ease of carrier drift.

Drift Current Density

Drift current density is proportional to velocity, density, and charge of carriers.

Jn = -qnvdn = -qnчn * E  for electrons
Jp = -qpv*dp = -qpчp * E  for holes

Therefore, the total drift velocity is:

Jdrift = Jn + Jp
  • According to Ohm’s law, the total drift velocity can be modeled as:
    • Jdrift = σE = E/ρ
    where σ is conductivity and ρ is resistivity

    Diffusion Current

  • The process by which charge carriers in a semiconductor move from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration. In other words, diffusion is particle movement (flux) in response to a concentration gradient.
  • Flux is defined as the number of particles crossing a unit area per unit time.
  • Diffusion process occurs in a semiconductor that is non-uniformly doped. The overall result is to erase the charge gradient. Collisions between particles and medium send particles off in random directions.
  • Current produced due to diffusion is diffusion current.

    Fick's First Law

  • It states that Diffusion Flux is proportional to the concentration gradient.
    • Fn = -Dn * (dn/dx)   for electrons
    Fp = -Dp * (dn/dx)   for holes
  • D is the diffusion coefficient. It measures the ease of carrier diffusion in response to a concentration gradient. D is limited by the vibration of lattice atoms and ionized dopants.
  • Diffusion current is the charge of the carrier times the flux of the semiconductor.
    • Jn = -qDn * (dn/dx)   for electrons
    Jp = -Dp * (dn/dx)   for holes
    Jdiff = Jp + Jn
